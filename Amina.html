Queen Amina

Warrior Queen of Zazzau

Life of Queen Amina

  • 1533 - Amina was born in Zazzau, an ancient city in Northern Nigeria.
  • 1566 - When her father died, her brother took over the throne, whilst she trained in military warfare.
  • 1576 - Her brother died and she suceeded the throne as the first Queen in what was a male-dominated society.

Reign of Queen Amina

  • She led her first military campaign just a few months after taking power. She would continue to personally fight in battles for the duration of her rule.
  • With an army of 20,000 soldiers and 1000 cavalry troops, she would go on to conquer neighbouring territory.
  • She expanded trade routes and commerce, helping to boost Zazzau's wealth and power in the region.

Legacy of Queen Amina

  • During and after her 34 year reign, she helped to solidify Zazzau's legacy in history
  • To mark and protect Zazzau, Queen Amina had the city surrounded by earthen walls. Some of which are still visible today.
  • Her memory represents the historic spirit and strength of womanhood

Amina’s legacy lives on even now. Her prowess and ability are also still celebrated today in songs and stories. They call her “Amina, a woman as capable as a man”.
